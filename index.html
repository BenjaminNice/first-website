What is Electricity?

Electricity is the flow of electric charge, usually carried by moving electrons in a conductor. It powers most of the modern world, from lighting our homes to running advanced machinery.

Fun Facts About Electricity

  • Lightning is a powerful discharge of electricity in the atmosphere.
  • The first practical electric light was invented by Thomas Edison in 1879.
  • Electricity travels at nearly the speed of light: 186,000 miles per second.
